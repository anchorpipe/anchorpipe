## Our Standards

### Examples of Behavior that Contributes to a Positive Environment

- ‚úÖ Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- ‚úÖ Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback

  - *Example: "I see what you're trying to solve. Have you considered using X approach because Y?"*

- ‚úÖ Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes

  - *Example: "You're right, my comment was dismissive. I apologize. Let me explain my concern more constructively."*

- ‚úÖ Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- ‚úÖ Showing empathy towards other community members
- ‚úÖ **Welcoming and mentoring newcomers**

  - *Example: "Great first issue! Here's a guide that might help you get started."*

- ‚úÖ **Assuming good intent and asking for clarification**

  - *Example: "I might be misunderstanding‚Äîcould you help me understand your reasoning?"*

- ‚úÖ **Celebrating others' contributions and achievements**

  - *Example: "This PR is excellent‚Äîreally clean solution to a tricky problem!"*

- ‚úÖ **Using inclusive language**

  - *Example: "folks" instead of "guys", "main" instead of "master"*

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- ‚ùå The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances
- ‚ùå Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks

  - *Example: "This code is garbage" vs. "This approach has performance issues because..."*

**Gray Areas?** When in doubt, ask yourself: *"Would I say/do this in a face-to-face conversation with a colleague I respect?"*

## Resolving Issues

We encourage community members to resolve minor conflicts directly when possible. Here's how:

### Self-Resolution (Encouraged for Minor Issues)

**Before reporting**, consider if you can address it directly:

1. **Assume good intent** - Most conflicts stem from miscommunication
2. **Communicate privately** - Reach out via DM or email: "Hey, your comment felt dismissive. Can we talk?"
3. **Focus on impact** - "When you said X, I felt Y" rather than "You are/did Z"
4. **Seek to understand** - "Help me understand your perspective"

**Example:**

> "Hi @user, I noticed you said my PR was 'a mess'. I'm happy to improve it, but that phrasing felt harsh. Could you share specific concerns?"

### When to Escalate

**Report immediately if:**

- You feel unsafe or harassed
- The behavior is severe (threats, doxxing, discrimination)
- Direct resolution attempts failed
- You're uncomfortable addressing it directly
- The behavior is repeated after being addressed

**It's always okay to report.** You won't be blamed for escalating.

## Reporting Violations

We take all reports seriously and will respond promptly. You have multiple options:

### How to Report

**Option 1: Private GitHub Report (Preferred)**

1. Go to [Security Advisories](https://github.com/anchorpipe/anchorpipe/security/advisories)
2. Click "Report a vulnerability"
3. Select "Code of Conduct Violation" as the type
4. Describe the incident

**Option 2: Direct Email**

- **Primary Contact:** conduct@anchorpipe.dev
- **Security Contact:** security@anchorpipe.dev (for security-related conduct issues)

**Option 3: Project Owner**

- Contact the project owner via [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/anchorpipe/anchorpipe/discussions) (private message)

### What to Include

To help us respond effectively, please include:

- **Your contact information** (if you're comfortable sharing)
- **Names** of people involved (reporter, accused, witnesses)
- **Description** of the incident with as much detail as possible
- **Context**: Where and when it occurred
- **Evidence**: Links to comments, messages, screenshots (if applicable)
- **Impact**: How this affected you or others

**You can report anonymously**, but it may limit our ability to follow up.

### What Happens Next

1. **Acknowledgment**: You'll receive confirmation within **24 hours**
2. **Investigation**: We'll review the report within **3 business days**
3. **Decision**: You'll be informed of the outcome within **7 business days**
4. **Action**: Appropriate measures will be taken per our [Enforcement Guidelines](#enforcement-guidelines)

**Your privacy is protected.** We will not share reporter information without consent, except when legally required.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

```

    ‚Üì (Continued violations)
‚è∏Ô∏è  Level 3: Temporary Ban
    ‚Üì (Severe/continued violations)
üö´ Level 4: Permanent Ban
```

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals Process

Decisions can be appealed if you believe:

- The investigation was incomplete or biased
- New evidence has emerged
- The consequence is disproportionate to the violation

### How to Appeal

1. **Submit your appeal** within **14 days** of the decision to: conduct@anchorpipe.dev
2. **Include**:
   - Original incident reference number
   - Reason for appeal
   - Any new evidence
3. **Review**: An independent reviewer (not involved in the original decision) will evaluate
4. **Decision**: Final decision within **14 days** of appeal submission

Appeals are reviewed by the project owner or an appointed independent reviewer.

**Appeals process applies to both reporters and accused parties.**

**When in doubt, report it.** We'd rather review a non-issue than miss a serious problem. You won't be penalized for good-faith reports.

If you're unsure, you can also:

- Ask anonymously in [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/anchorpipe/anchorpipe/discussions)

<details>
<summary><strong>What if I accidentally violated the CoC?</strong></summary>

We all make mistakes. If you realize you crossed a line:

1. **Apologize** publicly (if the violation was public)
2. **Self-report** to conduct@anchorpipe.dev explaining what happened
3. **Demonstrate changed behavior**

Self-reporting and genuine apologies are considered favorably.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Can I report something that happened a while ago?</strong></summary>

**Yes.** There's no statute of limitations, though the passage of time may affect available evidence and appropriate responses.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What if the person is a major contributor or maintainer?</strong></summary>

**Everyone is accountable**, regardless of contribution history or status. We investigate all reports impartially.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Will my report be truly confidential?</strong></summary>

**Yes**, with two exceptions:

1. We may need to share limited information with those directly involved in enforcement
2. We're required to disclose if legally mandated (e.g., court order)

We will NEVER:

- Share reporter details without consent
- Make public statements identifying reporters
- Retaliate or allow retaliation against reporters

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What if I disagree with a decision?</strong></summary>

You can [appeal the decision](#appeals-process) within 14 days.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How do you handle false or bad-faith reports?</strong></summary>

Intentionally false reports are a violation of this Code of Conduct. However:

- We distinguish between "mistaken" and "malicious"
- Good-faith reports that turn out to be unfounded are not penalized
- Patterns of bad-faith reporting will result in consequences

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Is there a difference between the Code of Conduct and the Security Policy?</strong></summary>

**Yes:**

- **Code of Conduct** - Addresses behavior and community interactions
- **Security Policy** ([SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)) - Addresses technical vulnerabilities

For reporting channel confusion, we'll route your report appropriately.

</details>
